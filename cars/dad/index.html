Vehicle shopping brief · Walla Walla, Washington · July 2026

Small crossover SUVs for an easy, premium-feeling in-town car

The goal is a vehicle that is clearly not a sedan, feels substantial and safe on occasional highway drives, but stays compact enough to park comfortably beside a Jeep Cherokee in the garage.

Mazda CX-30 2.5 S Premium
Most premium-feeling: narrowest body, refined interior and the most polished driving experience.
Toyota Corolla Cross Hybrid XSE
Best overall vehicle: strongest blend of hybrid efficiency, AWD, highway capability and local service.
Buick Encore GX Avenir
Smallest overall: nearly five inches shorter than the Toyota, with an upright, premium-oriented cabin.

Shopping goals

  • Compact exterior, especially length and width.
  • Easy garage parking and everyday maneuvering.
  • More upright and SUV-like than a sedan or wagon.
  • Quiet, comfortable and mature, not cheap or juvenile.
  • Safe and fully capable for occasional highway use.
  • Hybrid preferred, but not at the expense of size or quality.
  • Convenient service in Walla Walla is a major advantage.
What to call this category
Small crossover SUVs

“Subcompact crossover SUV” is the more technical label for the Buick and Mazda. The Toyota sits near the border between subcompact and compact, but “small crossover SUV” is the clearest phrase for all three.

How the three differ

Each vehicle fits the brief in a different way. The Mazda emphasizes premium feel and compact width. The Toyota emphasizes hybrid efficiency, AWD and convenient local service. The Buick emphasizes the shortest overall footprint and a more upright luxury-SUV presentation.

Mazda CX-30Premium cabin, narrowest body and the most polished driving experience.
Toyota Corolla Cross HybridHybrid efficiency, standard AWD and factory service in Walla Walla.
Buick Encore GX AvenirShortest length, upright proportions and traditional premium styling.
Best garage check: Measure the usable bay width with the Jeep parked normally, then compare each vehicle’s body width and door-opening clearance.
